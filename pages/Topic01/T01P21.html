Signs of infection can be subtle and non-specific, and often they overlap with other clinical conditions.

Respiratory Distress
  • Apnea
  • Tachypnea
  • Retractions
  • Nasal flaring
  • Grunting
  • Cyanosis
  • Need for oxygen or increase in oxygen and/or ventilator support
 
 
Abnormal Temperature
  • Hypothermia (≤ 36.4° C; 97.5° F) - more common
  • Hyperthermia (≥ 38° C; 100.4° F) - less common
  • Persistent fever
 
Gastrointestinal Signs
  • Poor feeding
  • Disinterest in feeding
  • Weak suck
  • Vomiting
  • Abdominal distension
  • Diarrhea
 
Cardiovascular Signs
  • Tachycardia
  • Bradycardia
  • Hypotension
  • Poor perfusion, prolonged capillary refill time
  • Skin mottling
  • Pale or gray skin color
 
Abnormal Neurologic Status
  • Irritability
  • Increased sleepiness
  • Lethargy
  • Hypotonia
  • Seizures
