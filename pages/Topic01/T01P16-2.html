The green color represents bacterial invasion

The terms intraamniotic infection and chorioamnionitis are often used interchangeably.
  • Not all chorioamnionitis is caused by infection
Intraamniotic infection
  • Most commonly caused by bacteria that ascend into the amniotic cavity from the vagina
  • Causes inflammation of any combination of the amniotic fluid, placenta, fetus, fetal membranes, or decidua

Suspected or confirmed Triple I criteria include a maternal fever with one or more of the following:
  • Fetal tachycardia, > 160 beats per minute for 10 minutes or longer.
  • Maternal white blood cell count greater than 15,000, in the absence of corticosteroids.
  • Purulent cervical discharge, by speculum exam.
  • Evidence of microbial invasion of the amniotic cavity by biochemical or microbiologic analysis.
A markedly elevated maternal temperature > 39 degrees C., even in the absence of other signs may also indicate intraamniotic infection.
